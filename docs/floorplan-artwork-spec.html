Floorplan artwork specification

Mandatory requirements for exhibition floorplan files supplied to Blueprint Event Company. Files that do not meet these requirements cannot be used in the interactive floorplan and will be returned.

1 — Why this exists Findings from LEX26

The LEX26 file could not be read reliably by software. Every stand number on the plan was unrecoverable, and the software had to guess which rectangles were stands. These are the measured findings from that file.

The single most damaging item

Converting text to outlines. It is irreversible: once a number becomes a set of shapes, the information is gone from the file. Nothing in post-production recovers it, and every downstream problem — wrong areas, wrong stand counts, manual re-keying — follows from it.

2 — Text Clauses R1–R3

R1

Never convert text to outlines, curves or paths

Every stand number, area figure and exhibitor name must remain live, selectable text in the delivered file. Do not apply Create Outlines (Illustrator), Convert to Curves (CorelDRAW), Flatten, or Explode Text (AutoCAD).

A quick check: open the file and try to select a stand number with the text tool. If you cannot place a cursor in it, the file fails this clause.

BecauseThis is the fault that made LEX26 unusable. All 5,970 text shapes arrived as outlines and not one stand number could be read.
R2

Never rasterise any part of the drawing

No stand, number, dimension or hall feature may be delivered as a bitmap, embedded image, or flattened image layer. The only permitted raster content is sponsor and exhibitor logos, subject to R3.

BecauseRasterised regions are unreadable and unclickable. A customer cannot select a stand that exists only as pixels.
R3

Must keep logos clear of stand boundaries

Sponsor logos may be placed images, but must sit in their own dedicated box and must not overlap any stand rectangle or any stand label.

NoteLEX26 handled this correctly — all 5 logo images sat in their own boxes. No change needed.

3 — Stand geometry Clauses R4–R8

R4

Must draw exactly one closed shape per stand

Each sellable stand is one rectangle or one closed polygon. One shape must never cover two or more stands, even where they share a wall and are drawn as a block with a dividing line on top.

BecauseIn LEX26 one rectangle covered stands 410 and 509. The software sold it as a single 59 m² unit that does not exist.
R5

Never stack duplicate or overlapping shapes

No two stand shapes may overlap. Do not leave an older shape underneath a newer one, and do not draw a block rectangle behind individually drawn stands.

BecauseFour stands in LEX26 existed twice, and one block rectangle sat behind two real stands. Counts and areas were wrong in both directions.
R6

Never leave zero-area or sliver geometry

Delete construction lines, collapsed rectangles and stray shapes. Any shape narrower than 0.5 m in either direction must not carry a stand style.

BecauseA 0.1-unit-tall rectangle in LEX26 was counted as an available stand — invisible on screen, but inflating the availability figure shown to customers.
R7

Must close every stand shape

Stand outlines must be closed polylines, not four separate line segments. Open paths cannot be filled or hit-tested.

R8

Must draw to true scale

Stand geometry must be at real-world scale, and the drawing must state its units and scale. The printed area figure must match the drawn geometry.

BecauseWithout a stated scale, areas can only be estimated. LEX26 areas were derived from a guessed conversion factor and every price was calculated from it.

4 — Label placement Clauses R9–R11

Label position is how software works out which text belongs to which stand. The convention below is already used on your plans and must be applied consistently.

412 Acme Lubricants Ltd 30
number top-left · exhibitor name centre · area bottom-right
R9

Must place each label in its fixed position

Stand number in the top-left. Area figure in the bottom-right. Exhibitor name centred. No other text may appear inside a stand.

R10

Must keep every label inside its own stand

A label must sit fully within the boundary of the stand it describes. Where a stand is too small, use a leader line to a label outside the plan area rather than letting text spill into a neighbour.

BecauseSoftware assigns text to a stand by asking which stand it sits inside. A number that overhangs is assigned to the wrong stand or to none.
R11

Must give every stand a unique number

Stand numbers must be unique across the entire show, not merely within a hall. Numbers must not be reused between shows within the same year.

5 — Colour and layers Clauses R12–R14

R12

Must use exactly one fill per status

Use these exact values. Do not introduce near-identical variants.

MeaningFillNotes
Available stand#FFFFFFPure white only
Taken stand#FCDF6D
Facility (catering, toilets, lounges)#60ABBFMust never be white
Structure (walls, conference rooms)#122830
BecauseLEX26 used three different near-whites for stands (#FFFFFF, #FBFBF7, #FAFBFB) and also used pure white for logo boxes. Three real stands were invisible to the software, and facility boxes were nearly sold as stands.
R13

Must separate content onto named layers

Minimum layer set, named exactly:

STANDS        stand outlines only
STAND-NUMBERS number text only
STAND-AREAS   area text only
EXHIBITORS    exhibitor name text only
FACILITIES    catering, toilets, lounges
STRUCTURE     walls, halls, conference space
LOGOS         placed images
ANNOTATION    titles, keys, arrows

Nothing else may sit on STANDS. In particular, facility boxes and logo backing boxes must not.

R14

Should avoid transparency and clipping masks on stands

Stand fills should be flat and fully opaque. Transparency, gradients, blend modes and clipping masks on stand geometry make colour detection unreliable.

6 — Companion data file Clause R15

R15

Must supply a stand schedule alongside the drawing

A CSV or Excel file listing every stand, issued with each drawing revision and matching it exactly.

stand_number,area_sqm,hall,status,exhibitor
412,30,1,available,
414,30,1,taken,Acme Lubricants Ltd
416,24,1,taken,Northern Oils BV

status is one of available, taken, reserved. exhibitor may be blank.

Why this matters more than anything else hereThe schedule is the authoritative record. With it, stand numbers and areas are read directly and no interpretation of the drawing is required. It takes minutes to export and removes every remaining risk in this document.

7 — Export and delivery Clauses R16–R18

R16

Must deliver in one of these formats

  • SVG — with live text. In Illustrator's SVG export set Type to SVG, never Convert to outline. Do not tick Preserve Illustrator Editing Capabilities.
  • DWG or DXF — with text as TEXT or MTEXT entities, never exploded to geometry.
  • PDF — with embedded fonts and selectable text, as a secondary format only.

A flattened PNG or JPEG is not an acceptable delivery format under any circumstances.

R17

Must keep geometry untransformed

Apply no scaling, rotation or group transforms to stand shapes at export. Coordinates must be baked into the geometry so a stand's position can be read directly.

R18

Must name files by show and revision

LEX26_Floorplan_v03.svg
LEX26_StandSchedule_v03.csv

The drawing and its schedule must always carry the same version number. Never re-issue a changed drawing under an existing version number.

8 — Acceptance checks Run before you send

Each delivery is checked automatically on receipt. A file failing any check is returned. Running these yourself first will save a round trip.

What happens on receipt

The file is parsed and every check above is run automatically. You will receive either a confirmation, or a report naming the specific stands and coordinates that failed. The check takes a few minutes, so a corrected file can usually be turned around the same day.

If only one thing changes

Keep the text live and send the stand schedule. Those two alone remove the great majority of the risk, and everything else in this document becomes a refinement rather than a rescue.
